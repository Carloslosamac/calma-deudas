---
name: CTR title & meta rule
description: Mandatory SERP CTR optimization (study top 10, beat them) for title/description on every page, including emoji + decorative separators
type: design
---
On every generated/edited page, optimize `title` and `metaDescription` for SERP CTR: study the real top 10 results for the target keyword, integrate and beat them. title <60 chars, description <160 chars, each unique. NO "| Calma"/branding in titles (wastes chars, no CTR gain — blog posts pass `appendSiteName={false}`). Each title must carry a differentiating hook, not a flat "guía/requisitos" pattern.

**Emoji + decorative separators (confirmed by competitor SERPs, e.g. Reparatudeuda "🥇 Ley segunda oportunidad 【 ACTUALIZACIÓN 2026 】"):** Google does render emoji and separators like 【 】 / «» in titles and descriptions.
- Title: at most 1 emoji at the start, thematic and sober (⚖️ legal, 🛡️ embargo, ✅ requisitos, 📉/💳 deuda, 🤝 negociación, 🌱 nueva etapa). Use 【 】 only when it frames real value (year/actualización/cifra), not on every title.
- Description: exactly 1 thematic emoji, counted within the 160 limit.
- Never depend on emoji/separator for meaning — Google strips them inconsistently; the text must read perfectly without them.
- Emoji and 【 】 count toward length; watch truncation.
